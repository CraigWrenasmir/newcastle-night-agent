Chapter 3: The Robot on the Deck

The giant machine stepped out of the metal crate.

CLANG.

Its heavy metal legs hit the deck of the ship.

Steam hissed from its joints.

Orange sparks flickered around its glowing red eye.

Agent Orion stayed perfectly still behind the cargo containers.

His drone hovered beside him.

Beep... beep...

"Shhh," Orion whispered.

The machine turned its head slowly.

Its red eye scanned across the deck.

Left.

Right.

Left again.

Orion could hear the soft hum of its motors.

"Headquarters," he whispered into his watch.

"I'm looking at a robot the size of a small car."

There was a crackle.

Then the voice replied.

"Our satellites confirm it's a security robot. It guards something valuable."

Orion peeked around the corner of the crate.

The robot walked slowly across the deck.

CLANG. CLANG. CLANG.

Each step shook the ship slightly.

Orion watched carefully.

He wasn't worried.

He was thinking.

"Every machine has a weakness," he murmured.

The robot's red eye glowed brighter.

Then suddenly...

It stopped.

Its head turned.

Straight toward Orion's hiding place.

The drone beeped nervously.

Orion froze.

The robot lifted one metal leg.

It began walking toward him.

CLANG. CLANG. CLANG.

"Alright," Orion whispered.

"Time for a little spy trick."

He reached into his pocket and pulled out a tiny round gadget.

It looked like a silver marble.

He rolled it quietly across the deck.

The marble stopped near the robot's feet.

The robot looked down.

The marble blinked.

FLASH!

A burst of bright light filled the deck.

Not loud.

Not dangerous.

Just very, very bright.

The robot's sensor flickered wildly.

Its red eye blinked.

Then blinked again.

The robot spun in a slow confused circle.

WHRRRRRR...

Orion grinned.

"Robots don't like surprise sunshine."

The drone zipped upward and scanned the deck.

Then it beeped excitedly.

BEEP BEEP!

Orion looked where it was pointing.

Behind the robot...

There was a large steel door built into the ship's deck.

And on the door was a warning sign.

RESTRICTED CARGO

Orion's eyebrows lifted.

"Well well," he whispered.

"That must be what our robot friend is protecting."

He carefully slipped out from behind the crates.

The robot was still turning in confused circles.

WHRRR... CLANG... WHRRR...

Orion tiptoed past it.

The drone hovered beside his shoulder.

Together they reached the heavy steel door.

Orion examined the lock.

It wasn't a normal lock.

It had glowing blue lights and a small screen.

"A puzzle lock," Orion said quietly.

"Those are my favourite."

But just as he reached for his tools...

The robot suddenly stopped spinning.

Its red eye flashed back to life.

And this time...

It was looking directly at him.
