Chapter 4: The Eraser

The robot's red eye glowed brighter.

Agent Orion stood beside the heavy steel door marked RESTRICTED CARGO.

The robot took a step toward him.

CLANG.

Another step.

CLANG.

The drone beeped nervously.

Orion sighed.

"Alright," he said quietly. "You win. Time for the big gadget."

He reached into his jacket and pulled out a strange device shaped like a small silver boomerang.

"This," Orion whispered, "is the Pulse Pusher."

The robot lifted its heavy metal leg.

Its eye flashed.

Orion pressed a button.

WHOOOMP!

A powerful wave of invisible force blasted from the gadget.

The robot staggered backwards.

Its metal legs scraped across the deck.

SKREEEECH!

Then...

CLANG!

It crashed into a stack of containers and fell onto its side.

The ship shook.

"Sorry about that," Orion said politely.

"But I need a minute."

The robot tried to stand again.

Its legs twitched.

But Orion had already turned back to the door.

He pulled out a small toolkit and connected a thin cable to the glowing puzzle lock.

Lights blinked.

Numbers flashed.

The drone hovered above his shoulder, watching.

"Almost... almost..." Orion muttered.

Then...

CLICK.

The door slid open.

Cold air drifted out of the container.

Orion stepped inside.

Rows of metal equipment lined the walls.

Strange machines.

Glowing power cells.

And in the very centre of the container...

A tall black device sat on a pedestal.

It looked like a smooth metal cylinder with a glowing ring of light around its middle.

A label was printed on the base.

THE ERASER

Orion read the small warning text.

His eyebrows slowly lifted.

"Oh... that's not good."

His watch crackled.

"Agent Orion," headquarters said. "We're detecting massive energy readings. What is it?"

Orion stared at the machine.

"It's worse than we thought," he said quietly.

"This device can erase anything it targets."

"Erase?" headquarters asked.

"Buildings," Orion said.

"Ships. Roads. Bridges."

He looked out toward the lights of Newcastle in the distance.

"Even a whole city."

The drone beeped softly.

Behind him, the robot was slowly climbing back to its feet.

Orion nodded.

"Well," he said calmly.

"That's definitely not staying here."

He grabbed a small charge from his belt and placed it on the base of the machine.

"Goodbye, Eraser."

He stepped back.

BEEP.

BEEP.

BOOOOM!

The charge exploded in a bright flash of sparks.

The machine cracked apart and collapsed into twisted metal.

Its glowing ring flickered once.

Then went dark forever.

Orion dusted off his jacket.

"Problem solved."

But then...

The robot stepped into the doorway.

Its red eye burned brighter than before.

The drone beeped urgently.

Orion sighed again.

"Alright," he said.

"Now we run."
