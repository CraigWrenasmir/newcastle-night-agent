Chapter 5: The Apartment on King Street

The city was quiet.

Late-night traffic rolled slowly through the streets of Newcastle.

Lights glowed in tall office windows.

A lone tram rattled down Hunter Street.

High above the city, on the corner of King Street and Watt Street, a small apartment window flickered on.

Agent Orion stepped inside.

He closed the door quietly and hung his jacket on a hook by the wall.

"Home sweet home," he muttered.

From the outside, the apartment looked completely normal.

Just a couch.

A kitchen.

A small bookshelf.

But Orion's home was anything but normal.

Hidden beneath the floorboards were secret equipment lockers.

Inside the bookshelf was a communications panel.

And stretched invisibly across the room were thin beams of light that formed Orion's laser security system.

The drone buzzed gently through the window and landed on the table.

Beep.

"Good work tonight," Orion said, giving it a small nod.

He poured himself a glass of water and sat down.

For the first time that night, he relaxed.

The Eraser was gone.

Newcastle was safe.

Or so he thought.

Suddenly...

BEEP. BEEP. BEEP.

A small red light flashed on the wall.

Orion froze.

His eyes narrowed.

"That's not good."

The laser security system had been triggered.

Someone was outside.

The drone lifted into the air.

Beep?

Orion walked quietly to the window and peeked through the curtain.

Down on the street below, several black vehicles had stopped outside the building.

Doors opened.

Men in dark uniforms stepped out.

More of them appeared from the alley.

They moved quickly and quietly toward the entrance.

Orion stepped back from the window.

"Well," he said calmly.

"That answers that question."

His watch crackled.

"Agent Orion," headquarters said urgently.

"We've intercepted a message. The people outside your apartment were sent by someone called..."

Orion finished the sentence.

"The Darkstalker."

There was a pause.

"You know the name?" headquarters asked.

Orion nodded slowly.

"Unfortunately."

Outside, heavy boots began climbing the stairs.

THUD. THUD. THUD.

Someone tried the apartment door.

The handle rattled.

Then a voice spoke through the hallway.

"Agent Orion."

The voice was cold.

"We know you're inside."

"Come quietly."

Orion smiled.

"Not tonight."

He walked to the centre of the living room and lifted a small rug from the floor.

Underneath was a metal panel.

He pressed his hand against it.

CLICK.

The floor slid open.

A hidden tunnel appeared beneath the apartment.

Orion climbed down the ladder.

The drone zipped down after him.

Just as the floor panel slid closed...

CRASH!

The apartment door burst open above them.

Guards rushed inside.

But Orion was already gone.

He ran through the underground tunnel beneath the streets of Newcastle.

Behind him, the drone beeped excitedly.

Orion smiled in the darkness.

"Well," he said.

"Looks like this mission isn't over yet."
