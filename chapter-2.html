Chapter 2: The Harbour Infiltration

The mysterious ship moved slowly through the dark water.

No lights.

No signal.

Just the quiet rumble of its engines as it drifted toward Newcastle Harbour.

Agent Orion watched from Nobbys Head.

Whatever was on that ship was big... and it was waking up.

He tapped his watch.

"Headquarters, I'm going in."

"Understood," the voice replied. "But be careful. We've detected armed guards on the vessel."

Orion smiled slightly.

"Good thing I came prepared."

He pulled a long black tube from his pack and fired it toward the harbour wall.

THWIP!

A grappling hook shot across the gap and latched onto a metal ladder near the waterline.

Orion clipped onto the cable.

"Time to pay them a visit."

He slid down the line, racing through the cold harbour wind until he landed silently on the rocks below.

From there he climbed onto the harbour walkway and sprinted along the edge toward the approaching ship.

Above him, the spy drone buzzed quietly, showing him everything on a tiny screen.

Three guards.

Walking the deck.

All wearing dark uniforms.

All carrying shock rifles.

Orion whispered, "Alright... let's dance."

He pulled a small disk from his pocket and tossed it onto the side of the ship as it drifted past.

The disk stuck instantly.

A second later...

FWOOOMP

A silent magnetic lift activated and pulled Orion straight upward like an invisible elevator.

He landed softly on the back of the ship's deck.

But the moment his boots touched the steel...

CLACK.

A guard turned.

"Hey! Who's there?!"

The man raised his rifle.

Orion moved faster.

From his sleeve he flicked out a compact stun baton.

The guard fired.

ZZZZT!

Blue energy blasted across the deck.

Orion rolled sideways behind a crate as sparks bounced off the metal.

Another guard shouted.

"INTRUDER!"

Footsteps thundered toward him.

Orion sprang up from behind the crate and charged forward.

The first guard swung his rifle.

Orion ducked.

WHACK!

The stun baton cracked against the rifle and sent it flying across the deck.

The guard tried to tackle him.

Bad idea.

Orion flipped him over his shoulder and tapped the baton lightly against the man's vest.

ZAP!

The guard dropped instantly, snoring.

But the other two guards were ready now.

They fired together.

ZZZZT! ZZZZT!

Energy blasts lit up the deck like blue lightning.

Orion ran across the crates, leaping between them as sparks exploded around his boots.

He reached into his pocket and tossed something small across the deck.

It rolled to the guards' feet.

"Uh... what's that?" one of them said.

The little device blinked.

FLASH!

A blinding burst of light filled the deck.

The guards stumbled back, covering their eyes.

Orion dashed forward like a shadow.

One quick strike...

WHACK

Another...

ZAP

Both guards collapsed onto the deck beside their friend.

Silence returned to the ship.

Orion dusted off his jacket.

"Three guards," he whispered to his watch. "Handled."

"Good work, Agent Orion," headquarters replied. "But we're picking up something else now."

Orion turned slowly toward the centre of the ship.

The giant shape he had seen earlier was now fully visible.

It stood on the deck like a massive metal crate with thick cables running into it.

And right now...

Those cables were glowing.

The crate began to open with a deep mechanical rumble.

KRRRRRRRRR...

Orion's eyes widened.

Inside the crate...

Something huge began unfolding.

Metal legs.

Rotating sensors.

A turret slowly rising upward.

"Agent Orion... that's not cargo," headquarters gasped over the radio.

Orion stepped back slowly.

"No," he said.

"That's a weapon."

The machine turned.

And its glowing red sensor locked directly onto him.

Orion sighed.

"Well..."

He spun the stun baton in his hand.

"This just got interesting."
